A Scientific Journey

Student → Engineer → Teacher → Student

My path to graduate school may be unusual, but I have been engaging in the scientific process throughout my studies and professional life.

High School: Alcohol Stoves

Alchol Stove Image: By Frank Behnsen, CC BY-SA 3.0

At Deerfield Academy, I used stats class as an opportunity to craft simple alcohol stoves out of pop cans and to compare their performance scientifically.

What is the optimal burner hole size for maximum fuel efficency? What's the optimal height of the stove to achieve needed burn time and minimum priming time?

My alcohol stove project was a nutshell of the scientific process - starting with question formulation, continuing with experimental design and data collection, and ending with interpretation and communication of the results.

College: Project-Based Research

As my coding abilities developed throughout college, I was eager to apply them to project-based and educational research. This took many forms throughout my time at the school.

  • In a software projects class, my partner and I examined how budding engineers could better prepare for technical interviews. We surveyed departing seniors and developed a website called Puzzledom which was designed to offer self-guided interview practice through logic puzzles and coding challenges.
  • Working for the Kraemer Lab, I implemented a mass-distributable online experiment designed to test how differences in physics intuition (as measured by time-bound responses to Free Body Diagram) correlated with SES, level of education, field of work, and other factors.
